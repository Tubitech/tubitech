Robot Name: Velox

#9694

The name of our robot, Velox, means "swift and rapid" in Latin. Velox weighs an average of 40 kg, excluding bumpers. The chassis is made of aluminum box profiles and polycarbonate sheets. The chassis measures 70*81 cm.

Robot Subsystems
1) Arm and Shooter (Combined)

The shooter system is used to launch the game piece into the amp and speaker. Squishy wheels are used to feed the game piece into the shooter, and shooter wheels are used to launch it. The squishy wheels and shooter wheels are manufactured by our team. Neo motors are used throughout this subsystem due to their light weight and small size. Two CIM motors connected to a Toughbox mini gearbox are used to lift the arm. A 5:1 reduction is achieved with pulleys connected to the Toughbox output and the arm's pivot shaft.

2) Intake System

Intake sistemi notanın sahadan robota alınabilmesi için kullanılmaktadır. Sistemde Intake millerinin döndürülmesi için 2 adet JE motor kullanılmaktadır. Millerin dönmesi sonucu millerin üzerinde bulunan squishy tekerler sayesinde nota sahadan alınıp önce arma oradan da shootera verilir. Intake sistemi için gereken squishy tekerler da takımımızın kendi üretimidir. Robotta intake sistemi notayı bumper’ın altından alacak şekilde tasarlanmıştır , bunun sebebi hem robotun boyutunu minimuma indirmek hem de dış darbelerden en az şekilde etkilenecek hale getirmektir. Intake sistemi iki çelik plakadan oluşmaktadır. Bu plakalar şaseye bağlıdır.

3) Drivetrain

Mk4i swerve modules are used for the movement of the vehicle. This makes our robot more agile. A total of 8 neo motors are used for the drivetrain.

4) Bumper

A bumper system is installed around the robot to make it more resistant to collisions and safer during matches. The bumper on Velox is manufactured in two pieces. One piece surrounds one edge of the robot, while the other surrounds three edges. For the bumper, cylindrical foams are covered with red and blue fabrics, and the team number is painted on the fabric.

5) Robot Electronics

Our robot is powered by a 12V 18Ah sealed lead acid battery. This battery is connected to the main breaker for safety. After this, the power goes to the PDH (Power Distribution Hub). From the PDH, power goes to the motor controllers, Roborio, Cancoders, and RPM (Radio Power Module). The power going to the motor controllers is then transferred to the motors. Our vehicle has 8 Spark Max motor controllers and 8 NEO brushless motors in the drive mechanism. Our vehicle has 4 Spark Max motor controllers and 4 NEO brushless motors in the Feeder and Shooter mechanism. Our vehicle has 1 Victor SPX motor controller and 2 JE motors in the intake mechanism. Our vehicle has 2 Victor SPX motor controllers and 2 CIM motors in the arm mechanism. The Cancoders on our vehicle measure the position of the wheels on the swerve modules. The Roborio is the brain of our robot and controls the vehicle and provides power to the through bore encoder. The radio connected to the RPM provides communication between the drive station and the robot. All robot electronics communication, except for the through bore encoder, is via CAN connection. The through bore encoder is connected to the Roborio's Digital ports.

6) Climbing

Our robot is climbed by 2 Cim motors. The motors are attached to aluminum plates and then to the chassis. A carbon fiber rod is attached to each motor, and a hook is attached to the end of the carbon fiber rod. The magnet-detachable hook detaches from the rod when subjected to a certain level of force, allowing it to climb.
