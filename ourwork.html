Our Projects

Our projects are designed to inspire and engage a diverse range of participants through science, technology, and creativity. Our project empowers our members, fosters a collaborative environment, and expands our reach to create a lasting impact in the robotics community and beyond. Our goal is to empower individuals, promote STEM education, and support the growth and development of future innovators.

FIRST THEME
1) FRCGPT

Every year, the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) introduces a new game based on a specific theme and publishes a comprehensive English rulebook detailing the game’s rules and regulations. However, the extensive nature of this document and its exclusive availability in English can create challenges for teams, particularly those whose primary language is not English. This can make it difficult for teams to quickly access and understand crucial information, potentially impacting their efficiency in preparing for the competition. To address this challenge, we developed FRCGPT—an AI-powered assistant designed to provide teams with fast, accurate, and Turkish-translated access to competition rules. Participants can directly consult FRCGPT whenever they have questions about game rules, receiving instant responses without the need to navigate through lengthy rulebooks. Instead of spending hours searching for specific rules and regulations, teams can now retrieve the information they need effortlessly, allowing them to manage their processes more efficiently. This innovation significantly enhances accessibility for all Turkish teams competing in FRC. By offering the rules in Turkish, FRCGPT not only accelerates access to information but also helps teams make more informed and strategic decisions based on a clear understanding of the rules. With this project, we are revolutionizing the way teams prepare for FRC. By streamlining the rule-learning process, FRCGPT enables teams to focus more on the core aspects of the competition, such as engineering design, problem-solving, and strategy development. Our goal is to eliminate language barriers, improve the competition experience, and empower teams to perform at their highest potential.

2) FRC Tech Talks

FRC Tech Talks is a project designed to bring the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) community together and facilitate knowledge sharing among teams. This live broadcast series features experienced FRC mentors and volunteers who have previously participated in the competition, providing teams with valuable insights and answers to their questions. Through this initiative, teams have had the opportunity to receive direct guidance from seasoned professionals, enabling them to find solutions to challenges they face and further develop their skills. By learning from those with extensive experience in FRC, teams can gain a deeper understanding of technical aspects, strategy development, and competition preparation. To ensure that the shared knowledge remains accessible, all live sessions have been recorded and converted into video content, creating a permanent learning resource for the FRC community. Teams and individuals can revisit these materials at any time, allowing them to benefit from the insights and expertise of mentors whenever needed. FRC Tech Talks has played a crucial role in enhancing communication and collaboration between teams, making information more accessible and fostering a culture of shared learning. By promoting experience exchange within the FRC ecosystem, this project has helped create a supportive and educational environment, ultimately strengthening the entire community.

4) Build Blog

The "Build Blog" project was created with the goal of sharing our team's journey through the design and construction of a robot, step by step. This initiative was a collaborative effort to provide valuable insights, guidance, and inspiration to the broader FIRST Robotics community, especially rookie teams who may be in the early stages of their own robotics journey. We began by documenting the entire process on our team’s website, providing a comprehensive resource for others to follow. The blog served as a transparent look into the different stages of building a competitive robot, covering every aspect from initial concept to the final product. This open-source approach was meant to guide new and developing teams, helping them understand the complexities of robotics and the nuances involved in constructing a robot for the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC).

FRC Dive
1) Coastal Cleanup

As part of our commitment to environmental sustainability, our team participated in the Coastal Cleanup Project, where we received environmental training from Istanbul Technical University (ITU) and TÜBİTAK officials before engaging in a hands-on field study. Following this training, we conducted a cleanup operation along the shores of the Marmara Sea, removing all visible pollutants from the coastline. Throughout the cleanup, we faced numerous challenges that highlighted the severity of environmental pollution, reinforcing our sense of responsibility toward nature. By collecting and properly sorting plastic, paper, glass, and other waste materials, we not only helped restore the area’s natural beauty but also contributed to the protection of marine life and ecosystems. This initiative was more than just a cleanup—it was a profound learning experience that deepened our awareness of water pollution’s impact on wildlife and strengthened our commitment to sustainable practices. Recognizing the importance of proper waste management and recycling, we have become more conscious of our daily habits and are now more dedicated than ever to preserving our environment and protecting natural habitats. By taking part in this project, we have embraced a lifestyle in harmony with nature, understanding that true environmental action goes beyond one-time efforts and requires continuous dedication to sustainability.

2) Hosting Dr. Alper Evcen: A Deep Dive into Marine Conservation

Our school had the honor of hosting Dr. Alper Evcen, a Senior Researcher and Marine Biologist at TÜBİTAK, who provided invaluable insights into water pollution, marine life, and ecosystem dynamics. During his presentation, Dr. Evcen emphasized the critical role of water as a fundamental resource for life, shedding light on the detrimental effects of water pollution on our environment and the challenges marine species face. He also explored the importance of maintaining ecological balance and shared crucial knowledge on the sustainable management of water resources. This session was an eye-opening experience for students, offering them a deeper understanding of natural resource conservation and ecological stability. By raising awareness of environmental issues, Dr. Evcen’s talk helped shape the students' perspectives on sustainability, inspiring them to take action in protecting our planet for future generations.

3) Water Footprint Calculation

In this project, we developed a website to calculate water footprints and raise awareness about water consumption. The website featured a 17-question survey designed to assess participants' water usage habits and their consumption patterns across different areas. We first analyzed the responses to evaluate participants' awareness levels regarding water usage. The same survey was also conducted within our FRC team, allowing us to gather baseline data. Based on these findings, we provided in-depth training to our team members on reducing their water footprint. Following the educational sessions, participants retook the survey. The results showed a significant improvement in awareness and a reduction in water footprint levels. This initiative not only strengthened environmental consciousness within our team but also encouraged long-term behavioral changes towards more efficient water usage.

More Equal World
1) Star Girls of STEAM

The "Star Girls of STEAM" project, supported by academician Emine MANDAL, wife of the TÜBİTAK president, aims to bridge educational inequality by providing comprehensive training for 8th-grade female students across Turkey. Through weekly lessons, we supported students from diverse regions, helping them prepare for the High School Entrance Exam, scientific olympiads, and various academic projects. Additionally, mentorship in arts and FRC was provided to encourage their growth in both technical and creative fields. By reaching over 6,000 female students, this initiative has offered equal opportunities in education and empowered young girls to pursue ambitious academic goals. Through this project, we continue to inspire and support the next generation of female leaders in STEAM fields.

2) EquiQuery

Unconscious gender biases persist in everyday language, often through idioms, proverbs, and expressions that reinforce male dominance and undermine women. To combat this issue, we developed EquiQuery, an AI-powered tool that detects and regulates sexist language in texts. EquiQuery can be used in education, media, content creation, and communication to promote more inclusive and respectful language. By identifying stereotypical or divisive phrases, it helps reshape societal perceptions and contributes to gender equality. With widespread adoption, EquiQuery has the potential to raise awareness and foster a culture of inclusivity, ensuring that language becomes a tool for empowerment rather than discrimination.

3) Women’S TEAM

The "Women’S TEAM" project, closely followed by the TÜBİTAK president, was designed to shed light on the experiences of female scientists and researchers in the world of STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics). By conducting in-depth interviews with female researchers at TÜBİTAK, we created a powerful publication that highlights both the challenges and triumphs of women in science. The primary goal of this project was to provide an invaluable platform for experienced female scientists to share their insights with younger generations. By showcasing their journeys, achievements, and contributions, we aimed to empower young women to break barriers and pursue careers in science and technology. To maximize its impact, the magazine was distributed to female students in the schools we visited, ensuring that these inspiring stories reached the next generation of female scientists. By providing role models and real-life success stories, we encouraged young girls to see themselves as future engineers, researchers, and innovators.

4) Little Bodies, Big Dreams

As part of our commitment to inclusive education, we organized the "Little Bodies, Big Dreams" project for special education students at İnönü Middle School. The goal was to provide these students with a chance to experience interactive learning and explore various fields within STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics). Throughout the project, we engaged students in a variety of activities designed to enhance their creativity, teamwork, and interest in science. They participated in fun activities like painting and singing, which allowed them to express themselves artistically. We also introduced them to curling, which promoted teamwork and physical coordination. Additionally, the students had the opportunity to carry out simple science experiments in the school laboratory, helping them understand scientific concepts in a hands-on way. We also demonstrated last year’s FRC robot, giving them a glimpse into the world of robotics and technology. To promote environmental awareness, we also organized a session on water conservation, teaching students about the importance of saving water and protecting the environment. By incorporating these activities, we were able to create a learning environment where students felt supported and excited to explore new things.

5) ROBO-ARENA Card Game

The Robo-Arena Card Game was developed as an interactive board game designed to engage and educate children who have overcome cancer at LÖSEV (Turkish Foundation for Children with Leukemia). The game was created with the goal of instilling a love of science in these children, as well as providing an opportunity for fun and learning in an inclusive and supportive environment. Robo-Arena Card Game consists of a series of cards featuring questions from various fields of science, art, and security.

6) Blue Depths in Braille

In collaboration with the Deniz Yaşamını Koruma Derneği (Marine Life Conservation Association), a specialized booklet was developed to introduce visually impaired individuals to the underwater world. This project was carried out in partnership with marine scientists specializing in aquatic life, marine pollution, and the Marmara Sea. To ensure accessibility, cooperation was established with the TÜRGÖK Foundation (Turkey’s Library for the Visually Impaired) to produce materials in Braille alphabet. These materials were then printed and distributed to visually impaired individuals. Additionally, with the support of TÜRGÖK, the booklets were sent to 18 different schools catering to visually impaired students. This initiative contributed to raising awareness about marine ecosystems and environmental issues while promoting inclusivity in education. By providing equal access to knowledge, the project aimed to foster a deeper understanding of marine life among visually impaired individuals.
